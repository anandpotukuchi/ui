Vishwakosh
Reimagining the Encyclopedia for a Mobile-First Generation

Project Type: Personal UX/UI Project
Duration: November 2021 – December 2021
Role: UX Designer, UI Designer
Platform: Mobile App (Android)
Status: Concept Project

Overview

Vishwakosh (विश्वकोश) was my first independent UX/UI project after completing my design certification.

The idea emerged from a simple observation:

Accessing historical and cultural information on mobile devices often feels fragmented, text-heavy, and difficult to explore.

Most knowledge platforms were either designed primarily for desktop users or lacked structured discovery mechanisms that encouraged exploration.

Vishwakosh aimed to create a mobile-first digital encyclopedia focused on Indian history, dynasties, historical documents, and educational content.

Problem

Students and history enthusiasts frequently encounter challenges when researching historical topics:

Information scattered across multiple sources
Limited contextual discovery
Difficult navigation between related subjects
Poor mobile reading experiences
Lack of access to primary historical documents

The challenge was to design a system that made learning feel exploratory rather than academic.

Goal

Design a digital encyclopedia that would allow users to:

Discover historical content
Search topics efficiently
Browse historical eras and regions
Access archival documents
Read long-form educational articles
Navigate complex knowledge structures on mobile devices
Research & Inspiration

The project was influenced by:

Wikipedia
Britannica
Google Arts & Culture
Museum archive systems
Educational publishing platforms

Key observations:

Existing Platforms	Limitation
Wikipedia	Information overload
Britannica	Limited exploration
Academic archives	Poor mobile usability
Government repositories	Difficult navigation

The opportunity was to combine discovery, search, and education into a single mobile experience.

Visual Design
Brand Identity

The name Vishwakosh (विश्वकोश) was chosen because it directly translates to:

Encyclopedia / Treasury of Knowledge

The branding intentionally used Devanagari script to establish a distinctly Indian identity.

Color System
Primary Color

Deep Blue

Used to communicate:

Trust
Knowledge
Authority
Stability
Typography

The design used a minimal editorial approach:

Large headings
High contrast hierarchy
Spacious layouts
Content-first presentation

The objective was to support long-form reading rather than visual decoration.

Key Features
1. Featured Content

The homepage highlighted a featured historical article.

Purpose:

Encourage discovery
Surface curated content
Create a starting point for exploration
2. Search Experience

Users could search historical topics directly.

Search results displayed:

Dynasty Name
Historical Description
Time Period
Founder Information

This helped users evaluate relevance before opening entries.

3. Dynasty Profiles

Each historical entity contained:

Overview
Government
Timeline Information
Related Topics

The tab-based structure allowed large amounts of information to be organized efficiently.

4. Discovery Section

Users could browse content through filters such as:

Historical Era
Geographic Region

This transformed learning from search-driven to exploration-driven.

5. Document Archive

One of the more ambitious features was the inclusion of historical documents.

Users could:

Browse archives
Open document records
View metadata
Read source materials

This feature aimed to connect educational content with primary references.

6. Articles

A separate editorial section provided:

Long-form content
Essays
Educational explainers
Contextual reading

This differentiated encyclopedia entries from narrative content.

Challenges
Designing for Depth

Historical information is inherently complex.

The challenge was balancing:

Depth of information
Simplicity of navigation

without overwhelming users.

Mobile Constraints

Large amounts of content needed to fit within a small screen.

Solutions included:

Progressive disclosure
Tab navigation
Content chunking
Search-first access
Limited Resources

As a solo learning project:

No user testing
No development team
No content specialists

Many decisions were based on design principles and competitive analysis.

What Worked Well
Strong Information Architecture

The structure scaled well across:

Articles
Dynasties
Documents
Search-Centric Navigation

Search became a natural entry point for users unfamiliar with historical topics.

Clear Content Hierarchy

Reading experiences remained focused and uncluttered.

Modular System

The architecture could easily expand to include:

Maps
Timelines
Multimedia
Knowledge graphs

Outcome

Although Vishwakosh remained a concept project, it became an important milestone in my design journey.

It was the first project where I explored:

Information architecture
Search systems
Content organization
Mobile reading experiences
Knowledge discovery patterns

Many of the principles developed during Vishwakosh later influenced larger ecosystem projects involving service design, platform architecture, and connected user journeys.
